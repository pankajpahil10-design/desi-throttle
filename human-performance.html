Questions from Oxford Aviation Books | Home

1. Hypoxia means:

a. Low blood sugar
b. Lack of oxygen
c. High blood pressure
d. Low heart rate
Answer: b. Lack of oxygen to body tissues

Hypoxia = oxygen deficiency at cellular level.

2. First symptoms of hypoxia:

a. Blue lips
b. Euphoria + impaired judgment
c. Unconsciousness
d. Headache
Answer: b. Euphoria and impaired judgment

Cerebral hypoxia first affects judgment/behavior.

3. Hypoxia most severe at:

a. Ground level
b. 10,000ft
c. Above 12,500ft without O2
d. 5,000ft
Answer: c. Above 12,500ft without supplemental O2

CAR requires O2 above 12,500ft for extended flights.

4. Hyperventilation causes:

a. Increased CO2
b. Decreased CO2 (respiratory alkalosis)
c. Normal CO2
d. Increased oxygen
Answer: b. Decreased CO2 (respiratory alkalosis)

Over-breathing reduces CO2, causes dizziness, tingling.

5. SCUBA diving - minimum wait before flying:

a. 6 hours
b. 12 hours
c. 24 hours
d. 48 hours
Answer: c. 24 hours (after non-decompression diving)

Wait 24hrs to avoid DCS before flying.

6. Decompression sickness is caused by:

a. Low oxygen
b. Nitrogen bubbles forming in blood
c. Carbon monoxide
d. Dehydration
Answer: b. Nitrogen bubbles in tissues/blood

DCS = dissolved nitrogen coming out of solution.

7. Middle ear pain during descent due to:

a. Expanding air
b. Contracting air (pressure differential)
c. Temperature change
d. Vibration
Answer: b. Middle ear pressure differential (cannot equalize)

Swallow/yawn to equalize pressure.

8. Spatial disorientation most common in:

a. Clear weather VMC
b. IMC (no visual references)
c. Night VMC
d. Landing
Answer: b. IMC or reduced visibility (no horizon)

With no visual reference, vestibular system misleads.

9. The Leans - type of spatial disorientation:

a. False sense of banking
b. Unrecognized slow roll
c. Inverted flight sensation
d. Coriolis illusion
Answer: b. Unrecognized slow roll

The leans = gradual unrecognized roll into bank.

10. Coriolis illusion occurs when:

a. Quick head movement in turn
b. Accelerating forward
c. Descending
d. Level flight
Answer: a. Quick head movement during prolonged turn

Head movement triggers false sense of rotation.

11. DVA stands for:

a. Diversified Visual Approach
b. Descent Vectors Approach
c. Disorientated Visual Approach
d. Delayed Visual Approach
Answer: c. Disorientated Visual Approach

Graveyard spin + Coriolis = DVA.

12. Carbon Monoxide in cockpit from:

a. Engine exhaust (leak)
b. Cabin pressurization
c. Electronics
d. Air conditioning
Answer: a. Exhaust leak (heater or engine)

CO = colorless, odorless, deadly. Check CO detector.

13. Blood alcohol limit for pilots:

a. 0.02%
b. 0.05%
c. 0.08%
d. Zero
Answer: c. 0.05% (India) / 0.02% USA

Rules vary by country - check CAR.

14. Alcohol affects:

a. Only at high altitude
b. Hypoxia + alcohol together worse
c. Only when drunk
d. Not relevant to flying
Answer: b. Combined with hypoxia = amplified impairment

At altitude, alcohol effects are magnified.

15. Fatigue in flight caused by:

a. Only physical work
b. Only stress
c. Physical + mental + environmental
d. Only lack of sleep
Answer: c. Physical, mental, and environmental factors

Multifactorial - workload, stress, noise, vibration, etc.

16. G-induced LOC (G-LOC) occurs when:

a. Positive G
b. Blood drains from brain (high +G)
c. Negative G
d. Low altitude
Answer: b. Blood pools in legs during high +G

G-LOC = +G induced loss of consciousness.

17. To prevent G-LOC:

a. Fly level
b. AGSM (anti-G straining maneuver)
c. Reduce speed
d. Descend
Answer: b. Anti-G Straining Maneuver (AGSM)

AGSM + G-suit maintains cerebral perfusion.

18. Vision at night - most sensitive:

a. Center of retina
b. Peripheral retina (rods)
c. Cone cells
d. Fovea
Answer: b. Peripheral retina (rod vision)

Night vision = look slightly off-center.

19. Red cockpit lighting preserves:

a. Day vision
b. Night adaptation (scotopic)
c. Color vision
d. Focus
Answer: b. Night adaptation (rod sensitivity)

Red light doesn't bleach rhodopsin.

20. Carbon monoxide poisoning symptoms:

a. Cherry red skin
b. Headache + dizziness + nausea
c. Blue lips
d. Trembling
Answer: b. Headache, dizziness, nausea, confusion

CO poisoning - treat with 100% oxygen.

21. Sleep inertia:

a. Waking up refreshed
b. Impaired performance after waking
c. Cannot sleep
d. Sleeping while flying
Answer: b. Impaired cognitive performance after sudden waking

Wait 10-15 min after waking before critical tasks.

22. Circadian rhythm disruption causes:

a. Better performance
b. Jet lag - fatigue, insomnia
c. No effect
d. Better sleep
Answer: b. Jet lag - fatigue, sleep disturbance

Circadian = body clock. Shift work/jet lag affects performance.

23. Effect of +G on vision first:

a. Complete blackout
b. Tunnel vision (grey out)
c. No effect
d. Double vision
Answer: b. Tunnel vision (grey out)

Grey out ‚Üí Black out ‚Üí G-LOC sequence.

24. Vision - useful field of view:

a. Wide scanning
b. Central vs peripheral - scan technique
c. Narrow focus only
d. One eye
Answer: b. Scan with central and peripheral vision

Effective scanning = look, don't stare.

25. Stress in cockpit:

a. Always bad
b. Manageable level can improve performance
c. Should be ignored
d. Not relevant
Answer: b. Moderate stress improves performance (Yerkes-Dodson)

Too much stress = impaired. Manage with training.

26. Decision making - DECIDE model:

a. Detect, Estimate, Choose, Identify, Do, Execute
b. Detect, Estimate, Choose, Implement, Do, Evaluate
c. Decide, Evaluate, Create, Implement, Do, End
d. Direct, Examine, Check, Intervene, Decide, Execute
Answer: a. Detect, Estimate, Choose, Identify, Do, Execute

DECIDE = systematic decision making in CRM.

27. CRM aims to:

a. Replace technical skills
b. Improve teamwork, communication, decision making
c. Replace pilots with automation
d. Increase workload
Answer: b. Crew Resource Management - improve safety through better human factors

CRM = crew coordination, communication, leadership.

28. Automation complacency:

a. Good thing
b. Over-reliance on automation, loss of manual skills
c. Flying by hand
d. Backup systems
Answer: b. Over-reliance on automated systems

Maintain manual flying skills, don't become dependent.

29. Startle effect:

a. Normal response
b. Initial reflex paralysis + degraded performance
c. Quick thinking
d. Training
Answer: b. Brief paralysis and degraded performance after sudden emergency

Brief freeze then controlled response - train for it.

30. Hazardous thought patterns:

a. Anticipation
b. Anti-authority, Impulse, Macho, Resignation, Follow-thru
c. Caution
d. Planning
Answer: b. AIM: Anti-authority, Impulse, Macho, Resignation, Follow-thru

Hazardous attitudes lead to errors - recognize and counter.

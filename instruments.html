Q1. The three basic flight instruments are:

a) Altimeter, Airspeed, VSI
b) Altimeter, Airspeed, Heading Indicator
c) Altimeter, Airspeed, Artificial Horizon
d) Altimeter, VSI, Turn Coordinator
Answer: b) Altimeter, Airspeed, Artificial Horizon

The three basic T-shaped flight instruments are: Altimeter (altitude), Airspeed Indicator (speed), and Artificial Horizon (attitude).

Q2. A pitot tube is used to measure:

a) Static pressure
b) Dynamic pressure
c) Differential pressure
d) Atmospheric pressure
Answer: b) Dynamic pressure

Pitot tube measures dynamic pressure (ram air pressure) which is used by the airspeed indicator to calculate airspeed.

Q3. Gyroscopic instruments use which principle?

a) Conservation of energy
b) Conservation of momentum (rigidity in space)
c) Bernoulli's principle
d) Pascal's law
Answer: b) Conservation of momentum (rigidity in space)

Gyroscopic instruments work on the principle of rigidity in space - a spinning rotor maintains its plane of rotation.

Q4. VSI (Vertical Speed Indicator) measures:

a) Rate of climb in feet per minute
b) Altitude
c) True airspeed
d) Ground speed
Answer: a) Rate of climb in feet per minute

VSI measures the rate of change of altitude (climb or descent) in feet per minute (fpm).

Q5. The static vent is used by which instruments?

a) Airspeed indicator only
b) Altimeter and VSI only
c) Altimeter, VSI, and airspeed indicator
d) Artificial horizon only
Answer: c) Altimeter, VSI, and airspeed indicator

All three pitot-static instruments use static pressure: Altimeter, Vertical Speed Indicator, and Airspeed Indicator.

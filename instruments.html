✈️ Flight Instruments (Oxford ATPL)

Questions from Oxford Aviation Books | Home

1. The three basic 'T' flight instruments are:

a. Altimeter, VSI, Heading Indicator
b. Altimeter, Airspeed Indicator, Artificial Horizon
c. Altimeter, Turn Coordinator, VSI
d. Airspeed, VSI, HSI
Answer: b. Altimeter, Airspeed Indicator, Artificial Horizon

These are the three basic 'T' flight instruments.

2. A pitot tube measures:

a. Static pressure
b. Dynamic pressure
c. Differential pressure
d. Atmospheric pressure
Answer: b. Dynamic (ram) pressure

Pitot tube measures dynamic pressure for airspeed calculation.

3. Gyroscopic instruments work on principle of:

a. Conservation of energy
b. Rigidity in space
c. Bernoulli's principle
d. Pascal's law
Answer: b. Rigidity in space

Gyros maintain plane of rotation due to angular momentum.

4. The Vertical Speed Indicator (VSI) measures:

a. Rate of climb in feet per minute
b. Altitude
c. True airspeed
d. Ground speed
Answer: a. Rate of climb in fpm

VSI displays rate of altitude change.

5. Static vent is used by:

a. Airspeed indicator only
b. Altimeter and VSI only
c. Altimeter, VSI, Airspeed
d. Artificial horizon only
Answer: c. All three pitot-static instruments

Static pressure used by altimeter, VSI, and airspeed indicator.

6. Altimeter is a:

a. Pressure gauge
b. Barometric altimeter
c. Radar altimeter
d. GPS altitude
Answer: b. Barometric altimeter

Pressure altimeter measures pressure to indicate altitude.

7. QNH is:

a. Aerodrome pressure
b. Altimeter setting to read altitude
c. Standard pressure
d. Regional pressure
Answer: b. Altimeter setting to read altitude

QNH = pressure reduced to MSL using ISA temps.

8. QFE is:

a. MSL pressure
b. Aerodrome pressure
c. Standard pressure
d. Regional pressure
Answer: b. Aerodrome pressure

QFE = reads zero on aerodrome runway.

9. Radar altimeter measures:

a. Pressure altitude
b. Height above terrain
c. Barometric altitude
d. Density altitude
Answer: b. True height above ground

Radar altimeter measures AGL using radio waves.

10. Mach meter measures:

a. IAS
b. TAS
c. Mach number (TAS / speed of sound)
d. Ground speed
Answer: c. Mach number

Mach = TAS / Local speed of sound.

11. Stick shaker warns of:

a. High speed
b. Approaching stall
c. Low fuel
d. Gear not down
Answer: b. Approaching stall

Shaker activates slightly above stall speed.

12. Artificial horizon senses:

a. Magnetic heading
b. Pitch and roll
c. Altitude
d. Airspeed
Answer: b. Aircraft pitch and roll attitudes

Attitude indicator shows pitch and roll.

13. Turn coordinator indicates:

a. Rate of turn only
b. Rate of turn and roll
c. Pitch only
d. Heading
Answer: b. Rate of turn and roll rate

Turn coordinator shows turn rate and roll information.

14. Heading indicator is:

a. Magnetic compass
b. Gyro-stabilized compass
c. GPS heading
d. Radar heading
Answer: b. Gyro-stabilized compass

Direction indicator uses gyro for stable heading.

15. Magnetic compass has lead/lag error:

a. On north/south headings
b. On east/west headings
c. In turning
d. At equator only
Answer: a. Maximum on N/S, zero on E/W

Lead/lag error max at north/south headings.

16. VOR indicator shows:

a. Distance to station
b. Radial FROM station
c. Both TO/FROM and deviation
d. Aircraft heading
Answer: c. TO/FROM and deviation from course

VOR shows whether flying TO or FROM station and CDI deflection.

17. DME measures:

a. Ground speed
b. Slant range distance
c. Altitude
d. Heading
Answer: b. Line-of-sight distance to station

DME shows slant range - direct distance to ground station.

18. ILS provides:

a. Distance only
b. Direction only
c. Both direction and distance
d. Weather
Answer: c. Localizer (horizontal) + Glideslope (vertical) + Markers

ILS provides full approach guidance.

19. GPS provides:

a. 2D position
b. 3D position + time
c. Altitude only
d. Heading only
Answer: b. 3D position + velocity + time (PVT)

GPS gives position, velocity, and time.

20. EFIS displays:

a. Only flight instruments
b. Flight and navigation information on screens
c. Engine parameters only
d. Radio frequencies only
Answer: b. Electronic Flight Instrument System - flight + nav

EFIS shows PFD, ND, engine, and system info on screens.

21. PFD shows:

a. Navigation map
b. Primary flight instruments (attitude, speed, altitude)
c. Engine gauges
d. Weather radar
Answer: b. Primary Flight Display - attitude, speed, altitude, heading

PFD shows all primary flight instruments electronically.

22. Radio altimeter operational range:

a. 0-500 ft AGL
b. 0-2500 ft AGL
c. Above 10,000 ft
d. Only for landing
Answer: b. Typically 0-2500 ft AGL

Radio altimeter used for approach and landing.

23. ADC provides:

a. Altitude only
b. Airspeed, altitude, temperature
c. Heading
d. Position
Answer: b. Air Data Computer - IAS, TAS, altitude, temperature, Mach

ADC processes pitot-static and temperature data.

24. Stall warning activates:

a. At stall speed
b. Above stall speed (before stall)
c. After stall
d. At Vne
Answer: b. Slightly above stall speed (warning before stall)

Stall warning gives alert before actual stall.

25. G-meter measures:

a. Speed
b. Load factor (G forces)
c. Altitude
d. Fuel
Answer: b. Load factor (G forces)

G-meter shows aircraft load factor during maneuvers.

26. Weather radar shows:

a. Air traffic
b. Precipitation intensity
c. Wind
d. Terrain
Answer: b. Precipitation and weather echoes

Weather radar detects rain, clouds, storms.

27. TCAS provides:

a. Weather info
b. Traffic advisory and resolution advisory
c. Navigation
d. Landing guidance
Answer: b. Traffic and Resolution Advisories (TA and RA)

TCAS detects nearby aircraft and provides collision avoidance.

28. FMS is:

a. Radio
b. Flight Management System - manages navigation, performance
c. Communication
d. Engine control
Answer: b. Flight Management System - integrates navigation, performance, autoflight

FMS manages flight planning, navigation, and performance.

29. Sat compass uses:

a. Magnetic field
b. GPS satellites
c. Gyro
d. Radio waves
Answer: b. GPS position to calculate heading

Satellite compass derives heading from GPS movement.

30. Integrated standby flight instrument:

a. Separate gauges
b. Single electronic standby display
c. Backup compass only
d. Not required
Answer: b. Single electronic standby showing attitude, speed, altitude

ISFI provides all standby flight info on one screen.

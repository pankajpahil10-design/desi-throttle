1. Great Circle represents:

a. Longest distance
b. Shortest distance between two points
c. Same latitude
d. Magnetic heading
Answer: b. Shortest distance

Great circle = shortest path on sphere.

2. 1 Nautical Mile equals:

a. 6080 feet
b. 5280 feet
c. 7000 feet
d. 6000 feet
Answer: a. 6080 feet (1852m)

1 NM = 1 minute of latitude.

3. Rhumb line represents:

a. Shortest distance
b. Constant bearing track
c. Great circle
d. Magnetic course
Answer: b. Constant bearing track

Rhumb line = constant heading, longer than great circle.

4. Convergence at poles is:

a. 0¬∞
b. 90¬∞ per 60 nm
c. Maximum
d. Equal at all latitudes
Answer: c. Maximum at poles

Meridians converge at poles - 1¬∞ per 60 nm at equator.

5. Speed/distance unit conversions:

a. 1 knot = 1.852 km/h
b. 1 knot = 1 km/h
c. 1 knot = 2 km/h
d. 1 knot = 1.5 km/h
Answer: a. 1 knot = 1.852 km/h

1 knot = 1 NM per hour = 1.852 km/h.

6. Solar time at Greenwich is:

a. LMT
b. UTC (Zulu)
c. IST
d. Local time
Answer: b. UTC (Zulu)

All aviation uses UTC.

7. Longitude measures:

a. North-South
b. East-West (0-180¬∞)
c. Altitude
d. Latitude
Answer: b. East-West (0-180¬∞ E/W)

Longitude = East-West position from Prime Meridian.

8. Latitude measures:

a. East-West
b. North-South (0-90¬∞)
c. Altitude
d. Longitude
Answer: b. North-South (0-90¬∞ N/S)

Latitude = North-South position from equator.

9. Earth's shape is:

a. Perfect sphere
b. Oblate spheroid
c. Flat
d. Oval
Answer: b. Oblate spheroid

Earth flattened at poles due to rotation.

10. 1¬∞ of latitude equals:

a. 1 nm
b. 60 nm
c. 100 nm
d. 1 km
Answer: b. 60 nm

1¬∞ latitude = 60 nm = 111 km.

11. Variation is:

a. Difference between true and magnetic
b. Difference between magnetic and compass
c. Compass error
d. Deviation
Answer: a. Difference between true and magnetic north

Variation = angular difference between True and Magnetic north.

12. Deviation is:

a. Magnetic variation
b. Aircraft magnetic interference
c. True heading
d. Compass error
Answer: b. Local magnetic interference in aircraft

Deviation = error due to aircraft magnetic fields.

13. Compass heading + deviation + variation =

a. True heading
b. Magnetic heading
c. Relative heading
d. Grid heading
Answer: a. True heading

CH + Dev + Var = TH.

14. Grid navigation uses:

a. Magnetic north
b. Grid north (map projection)
c. True north
d. Compass north
Answer: b. Grid north

Grid navigation uses arbitrary reference for polar areas.

15. Departure formula:

a. Dlong x cos(lat)
b. Dlong / cos(lat)
c. Distance x sin(lat)
d. Latitude difference
Answer: a. Dlong x cos(mean latitude)

Departure = change in longitude x cos(lat).

16. Mean Sun is:

a. Actual sun
b. Fictitious sun for uniform time
c. Apparent sun
d. Star
Answer: b. Fictitious sun moving at constant speed

Mean Sun = imaginary sun for mean solar time.

17. Equation of time is:

a. Apparent - Mean solar time
b. Mean - Apparent solar time
c. UTC - LMT
d. Longitude difference
Answer: a. Apparent - Mean solar time

Equation of time = difference between apparent and mean sun.

18. Sunrise/sunset calculated from:

a. Local time
b. UTC
c. LMT
d. IST
Answer: b. UTC (then convert to local)

Sun times calculated in UTC then converted.

19. LMT at longitude 75¬∞E:

a. UTC + 5
b. UTC + 5 hours
c. UTC - 5
d. Same as UTC
Answer: b. UTC + 5 hours (75¬∞ = 5 hours)

1¬∞ = 4 minutes, so 75¬∞ = 300 min = 5 hours.

20. Twilight types:

a. Civil only
b. Civil, Nautical, Astronomical
c. Morning only
d. Evening only
Answer: b. Civil (6¬∞), Nautical (12¬∞), Astronomical (18¬∞)

Three types of twilight below horizon.

21. Star identification requires:

a. Only altitude
b. Altitude and azimuth
c. Only azimuth
d. Time only
Answer: b. Altitude and Azimuth

Intercept method requires both altitude and bearing.

22. Position line from celestial:

a. Circle
b. Straight line (circle small section)
c. Hyperbola
d. Parabola
Answer: b. Small circle section treated as straight line

Celestial position line is part of a small circle.

23. Cock-sure rule for stars:

a. Stars visible to naked eye
b. Use brightest stars at twilight
c. Any star
d. Planets only
Answer: b. Bright stars visible during twilight

Cock's crow = brightest stars visible at dawn/dusk.

24. DR position is:

a. GPS position
b. Calculated from last known position + heading/speed/time
c. Actual position
d. Radar position
Answer: b. Dead reckoning - calculated position

DR = last position + course made good.

25. Wind triangle solves:

a. TAS only
b. Heading, groundspeed, wind correction angle
c. Fuel consumption
d. Altitude
Answer: b. Heading, groundspeed, and WCA

Wind triangle = TAS + W/V ‚Üí GS and Heading.

26. Maximum range cruise is:

a. Maximum speed
b. L/D max angle of attack
c. Minimum fuel flow
d. High altitude
Answer: b. At angle of attack for L/D max

Best range = fly at L/D max angle of attack.

27. 1 knot equals:

a. 1 nm per minute
b. 1 nm per hour
c. 1 km per hour
d. 1 mile per hour
Answer: b. 1 nautical mile per hour

1 knot = 1 NM/h.

28. ETP calculation assumes:

a. No wind
b. Constant wind
c. Tailwind all the way
d. No fuel consumed
Answer: b. Constant wind for both legs

Equal Time Point assumes constant wind on both legs.

29. PNR calculation:

a. Halfway fuel
b. Fuel remaining = fuel to destination
c. No alternate needed
d. Full fuel
Answer: b. Point where remaining fuel = fuel to destination

Point of No Return = can't return with reserves.

30. Map projection types:

a. Mercator only
b. Lambert, Mercator, Stereographic
c. Polar only
d. Only conformal
Answer: b. Lambert (mid-lat), Mercator (equatorial), Polar/Stereographic (polar)

Different projections for different latitudes.

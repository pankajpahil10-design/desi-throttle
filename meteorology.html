Questions extracted from Oxford Book 9 - Meteorology

1. What is the ISA sea level temperature?

a. 10¬∞C
b. 15¬∞C
c. 20¬∞C
d. 25¬∞C
Answer: b. 15¬∞C (288K)

International Standard Atmosphere temperature at MSL.

2. The standard lapse rate is:

a. 1¬∞C per 1000ft
b. 2¬∞C per 1000ft
c. 1.5¬∞C per 1000ft
d. 3¬∞C per 1000ft
Answer: b. 2¬∞C per 1000ft

ISA lapse rate in troposphere.

3. Which cloud is associated with thunderstorms?

a. Stratus
b. Cumulus
c. Cumulonimbus
d. Nimbostratus
Answer: c. Cumulonimbus (CB)

CB clouds produce thunderstorms, heavy rain, turbulence.

4. Approximate tropopause height at mid-latitudes:

a. 20,000ft
b. 35,000ft
c. 60,000ft
d. 10,000ft
Answer: b. 35,000ft (11km)

Varies with latitude - 35,000ft at mid-latitudes.

5. Wind flows from:

a. High to Low pressure
b. Low to High pressure
c. Equator to poles
d. Poles to equator
Answer: a. High to Low pressure

Wind flows from high to low pressure, deflected by Coriolis.

6. QNH is:

a. Aerodrome pressure
b. Altimeter setting to read altitude
c. Pressure at MSL
d. Runway pressure
Answer: b. Altimeter setting to read altitude

QNH = pressure reduced to MSL using ISA temperatures.

7. QFE is:

a. MSL pressure
b. Aerodrome pressure
c. Standard pressure
d. Regional pressure
Answer: b. Aerodrome pressure

QFE = pressure at aerodrome - altimeter reads 0 on runway.

8. What causes wind?

a. Temperature
b. Pressure difference
c. Humidity
d. Clouds
Answer: b. Horizontal pressure difference

Wind caused by pressure gradient force.

9. Coriolis force is maximum at:

a. Equator
b. Poles
c. 30¬∞ latitude
d. 45¬∞ latitude
Answer: b. Poles

Coriolis is zero at equator, maximum at poles.

10. In an anticyclone in Northern Hemisphere, wind flows:

a. Inward and clockwise
b. Outward and clockwise
c. Inward and counter-clockwise
d. Outward and counter-clockwise
Answer: b. Outward and clockwise

Anticyclone = high pressure, clockwise (NH), outward flow.

11. Stability of atmosphere when temperature decreases rapidly with height:

a. Stable
b. Unstable
c. Neutral
d. Conditionally unstable
Answer: b. Unstable

Steep lapse rate = unstable atmosphere.

12. Radiation fog forms when:

a. Ground cools rapidly at night
b. Warm air moves over cold ground
c. There is advection
d. Frontal activity
Answer: a. Ground cools rapidly at night

Radiation fog = ground radiates heat at night, clear skies.

13. Advection fog requires:

a. Calm winds
b. Light to moderate winds
c. Strong winds
d. No wind required
Answer: b. Light to moderate winds

Advection fog needs wind to transport moist air over cold surface.

14. Fog clears when:

a. Temperature drops
b. Wind increases
c. Wind decreases
d. Pressure rises
Answer: b. Wind increases

Mixing caused by wind disperses fog.

15. Warm front has:

a. Steep slope
b. Gradual slope
c. No slope
d. Variable slope
Answer: b. Gradual slope (1:300)

Warm fronts have gradual slope, cause widespread cloud/rain.

16. Cold front is usually:

a. 1:300 slope
b. 1:100 slope
c. 1:50 slope
d. 1:500 slope
Answer: c. 1:50 slope

Cold fronts are steeper (1:50), cause sudden weather.

17. Cloud type with continuous rain:

a. Cumulus
b. Stratus
c. Cumulonimbus
d. Cirrus
Answer: b. Stratus (Ns)

Nimbostratus produces continuous rain.

18. Thunderstorms are most severe in:

a. Cumulus stage
b. Mature stage
c. Dissipating stage
d. All stages equal
Answer: b. Mature stage

Mature stage has both updrafts and downdrafts, most severe.

19. Visibility reduced by haze is best improved by:

a. Temperature drop
b. Wind increase
c. Wind decrease
d. Pressure change
Answer: b. Wind increase

Mixing disperses haze particles.

20. Freezing rain occurs when:

a. Rain falls through cold air
b. Snow melts and refreezes
c. Rain falls through freezing level
d. Ice pellets form
Answer: b. Snow melts then refreezes

Freezing rain = snow melts in warm layer, freezes in cold layer.

21. Ice pellets are:

a. Frozen rain drops
b. Hail
c. Sleet
d. Snow pellets
Answer: a. Frozen rain drops

Ice pellets = rain that froze after falling.

22. VFR visibility requirement:

a. 1km
b. 3km
c. 5km
d. 10km
Answer: c. 5km (3nm)

Standard VFR visibility minimum is 5km.

23. METAR is:

a. Forecast
b. Actual weather report
c. Warning
d. Satellite image
Answer: b. Actual (routine) weather report

METAR = Meteorological Aerodrome Report (actual conditions).

24. TAF is:

a. Current weather
b. Forecast
c. Past weather
d. Warning
Answer: b. Forecast

TAF = Terminal Aerodrome Forecast (2-9 hour forecast).

25. SIGMET is:

a. Routine forecast
b. Significant weather warning
c. Air report
d. Satellite
Answer: b. Significant weather warning

SIGMET = Significant Meteorological Warning (CB, turbulence, icing).

26. Jet stream is found at:

a. Surface
b. FL100-200
c. FL250-400
d. FL500+
Answer: c. FL250-400 (near tropopause)

Jet streams are narrow bands of strong wind at high levels.

27. Average height of troposphere at equator:

a. 8km
b. 11km
c. 16km
d. 20km
Answer: c. 16km

Higher at equator (16km), lower at poles (8km).

28. Dew point is:

a. Temperature at which air becomes saturated
b. Temperature of clouds
c. Maximum temperature
d. Freezing level
Answer: a. Temperature at which air becomes saturated

Dew point = temperature at which relative humidity = 100%.

29. Relative humidity increases when:

a. Temperature increases
b. Temperature decreases
c. Wind increases
d. Pressure increases
Answer: b. Temperature decreases

Cooling air brings it closer to saturation.

30. Most dangerous icing in:

a. Cirrus
b. Cumulus
c. Cumulonimbus
d. Stratus
Answer: c. Cumulonimbus

CB has most severe icing - large supercooled water droplets.

31. ICAO standard pressure is:

a. 1000 hPa
b. 1013.25 hPa
c. 1015 hPa
d. 1020 hPa
Answer: b. 1013.25 hPa (29.92 inHg)

Standard sea level pressure for aviation.

32. Gradient wind is:

a. Wind due to pressure gradient only
b. Wind in curved isobars
c. Surface wind
d. Geostrophic wind
Answer: b. Wind in curved isobars

Gradient wind accounts for curvature in isobars.

33. Geostrophic wind is:

a. Wind near surface
b. Wind in straight isobars above friction layer
c. Wind in curved paths
d. Wind at low level
Answer: b. Wind in straight isobars above friction layer

Balance between PGF and Coriolis with straight isobars.

34. Friction layer is:

a. Above FL100
b. Below 1000ft AGL
c. At surface only
d. Above clouds
Answer: b. Below 1000ft AGL

Friction layer affects wind up to about 1000ft.

35. In Northern Hemisphere, surface wind is backed from geostrophic wind by:

a. 10¬∞
b. 20¬∞
c. 30¬∞
d. 45¬∞
Answer: c. 30¬∞

Surface wind is backed (veered) about 30¬∞ from geostrophic.

36. 1 Nautical Mile equals:

a. 5280 ft
b. 6080 ft
c. 6000 ft
d. 6500 ft
Answer: b. 6080 ft (1852m)

1 NM = 1 minute of latitude.

37. 1 knot equals:

a. 1 km/h
b. 1.852 km/h
c. 1.5 mph
d. 2 km/h
Answer: b. 1.852 km/h

1 knot = 1 NM per hour = 1.852 km/h.

38. D-Value is:

a. Density altitude
b. Deviation from ISA
c. Dew point depression
d. Drift angle
Answer: b. Deviation from ISA

D-value = difference between pressure altitude and density altitude.

39. Density altitude is:

a. PA corrected for temperature
b. PA corrected for humidity
c. Pressure height
d. Density of air
Answer: a. PA corrected for non-standard temperature

Density altitude = pressure altitude corrected for temperature.

40. Occlusion forms when:

a. Cold front catches warm front
b. Warm front catches cold front
c. Two cold fronts meet
d. Stationary front develops
Answer: a. Cold front catches warm front

Occlusion = cold front overtakes warm front.

41. Wing tip vortices are strongest with:

a. Light aircraft, low speed
b. Heavy aircraft, high speed
c. Heavy aircraft, low speed
d. Light aircraft, high speed
Answer: c. Heavy aircraft, low speed

Vortices strongest with high weight, low speed (takeoff/landing).

42. Mountain wave turbulence occurs:

a. On leeward side of mountains
b. On windward side
c. At mountain tops only
d. In valleys only
Answer: a. On leeward side of mountains

Lee waves form on downwind side of mountains.

43. Clear air turbulence (CAT) is found:

a. Near thunderstorms
b. At high levels near jet streams
c. Near mountains
d. At low levels
Answer: b. At high levels near jet streams

CAT occurs at high levels, often near jet stream boundaries.

44. Wake turbulence moves:

a. Upward
b. Down

Q5.

At standard atmosphere, what is the relationship between pressure altitude and density altitude?

  • a) Pressure altitude always > density altitude
  • b) Equal at ISA conditions
  • c) Density altitude always < pressure altitude
  • d) They are unrelated

‚úÖ Answer: b ‚Äî Equal at ISA

At ISA (International Standard Atmosphere):

  • Sea level pressure: 1013.25 hPa
  • Sea level temp: +15¬∞C
  • Lapse rate: -2¬∞C/1000ft

At ISA: Pressure Altitude = Density Altitude

Hotter than ISA: DA > PA (air less dense)

Colder than ISA: DA < PA

Q6.

What does RVR stand for, and how is it measured?

  • a) Runway Visual Range - measured by anemometer
  • b) Runway Visual Range - measured by transmissometer
  • c) Radar Visual Range - measured by radar
  • d) Required Visual Range - pilot's requirement

‚úÖ Answer: b

RVR (Runway Visual Range) is:

  • Distance a pilot can see down the runway
  • Measured by transmissometer or RVR sensor
  • Displayed in meters (not feet)
  • Used for Category I, II, III ILS approaches

Remember: RVR replaces visibility for precision approaches.

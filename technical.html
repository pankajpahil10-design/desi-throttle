Q1. Which type of engine is most commonly used in transport aircraft?

a) Piston engine
b) Turbojet
c) Turbofan
d) Turboprop
Answer: c) Turbofan

Modern transport aircraft use high-bypass turbofan engines for fuel efficiency and low noise.

Q2. The primary purpose of a wing is to produce:

a) Thrust
b) Drag
c) Lift
d) Weight
Answer: c) Lift

Wings produce lift by generating pressure difference due to camber and angle of attack.

Q3. Flaps are used to:

a) Increase speed
b) Increase lift and drag at lower speeds
c) Reduce weight
d) Improve engine cooling
Answer: b) Increase lift and drag at lower speeds

Flaps increase wing camber and surface area, providing more lift at lower speeds for takeoff/landing.

Q4. The four forces acting on an aircraft are:

a) Thrust, drag, lift, weight
b) Power, speed, altitude, direction
c) Pitch, roll, yaw, thrust
d) Engine, wings, fuselage, tail
Answer: a) Thrust, drag, lift, weight

These are the four fundamental forces in flight: thrust propels, drag opposes, lift supports, weight pulls down.

Q5. Ailerons control:

a) Pitch
b) Yaw
c) Roll
d) Throttle
Answer: c) Roll

Ailerons control roll (rotation about the longitudinal axis) by differential lift on wings.

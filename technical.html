✈️ Principles of Flight (Oxford ATPL)

Questions from Oxford Aviation Books | Home

1. Lift is produced by:

a. Thrust
b. Pressure difference
c. Weight
d. Drag
Answer: b. Pressure difference (Bernoulli's principle)

Lift = pressure difference between upper and lower wing surfaces.

2. Angle of Attack is angle between:

a. Chord line and airflow
b. Chord line and fuselage
c. Wing and horizontal stabilizer
d. Aircraft and ground
Answer: a. Chord line and relative airflow

AOA = angle between chord line and relative airflow.

3. Critical angle of attack is:

a. Angle for maximum lift
b. Angle where stall occurs
c. Zero lift angle
d. L/D max angle
Answer: b. Angle where stall occurs (regardless of speed/weight)

Critical AoA = stall angle (typically 15-20°).

4. Stall speed increases with:

a. Decreasing weight
b. Increasing weight
c. Higher altitude
d. Lower altitude
Answer: b. Increasing weight (and bank angle, load factor)

Vs increases with √load factor × weight.

5. Wing loading is:

a. Weight × Area
b. Weight / Wing Area
c. Lift / Drag
d. Thrust / Weight
Answer: b. Weight / Wing Area (W/S)

Wing loading affects stall speed and handling.

6. Aspect ratio is:

a. Chord / Span
b. Span² / Area
c. Area / Span
d. Thickness / Chord
Answer: b. Span² / Area (or Span/Chord)

High aspect ratio = long narrow wings (gliders).

7. Induced drag is caused by:

a. Wing tip vortices
b. Form resistance
c. Skin friction
d. Wave drag
Answer: a. Wing tip vortices (downwash)

Induced drag = price of lift, minimum at best L/D angle.

8. Parasite drag includes:

a. Induced drag only
b. Form drag + skin friction
c. Wave drag only
d. Lift-induced
Answer: b. Form drag + skin friction (parasitic)

Parasite drag = all drag except induced.

9. Best L/D ratio occurs at:

a. Lowest speed
b. Highest speed
c. Specific AoA (~4°
d. Stall angle
Answer: c. Specific AoA (~4° for typical wing)

Best L/D at one specific angle of attack.

10. Flaps increase:

a. Lift only
b. Drag only
c. Both lift and drag
d. Thrust only
Answer: c. Both lift and drag

Flaps increase camber and area = more lift AND more drag.

11. Swept wings are used to:

a. Increase lift
b. Decrease drag at high speed
c. Increase stability
d. Reduce weight
Answer: b. Reduce wave drag at transonic/supersonic speeds

Swept wings delay shock wave formation.

12. Vortex generators:

a. Reduce drag
b. Increase lift
c. Energize boundary layer / delay stall
d. Reduce weight
Answer: c. Energize boundary layer to delay separation

Vortex generators re-energize airflow, improve control at high AoA.

13. Ground effect reduces:

a. Lift
b. Induced drag
c. Weight
d. Thrust required
Answer: b. Induced drag (upwash reduces vortex strength)

Ground effect = reduced induced drag, lower stall speed.

14. Load factor in a 60° bank turn:

a. 1.0
b. 1.5
c. 2.0
d. 3.0
Answer: c. 2.0 (1/cos60° = 1/0.5 = 2)

Load factor = 1/cos(bank). At 60° bank = 2G.

15. True airspeed increases with altitude at same IAS because:

a. Air is denser
b. Air is less dense
c. Temperature decreases
d. Pressure increases
Answer: b. Air is less dense (same IAS = higher TAS)

TAS = IAS × √(ρ₀/ρ). Less density = higher TAS.

16. Boundary layer is:

a. High energy air near surface
b. Slow moving air near surface
c. Turbulent air above wing
d. Clear air
Answer: b. Thin layer of slow moving air near surface

Boundary layer starts laminar, becomes turbulent.

17. Camber of wing is:

a. Thickness ratio
b. Curvature of upper/lower surface
c. Wing span
d. Chord length
Answer: b. Curvature (difference between upper/lower surface)

Camber determines lift coefficient at zero AoA.

18.-dihedral provides:

a. Longitudinal stability
b. Lateral stability (roll)
c. Directional stability
d. Propulsive force
Answer: b. Lateral (roll) stability

Dihedral increases lateral stability - wing drops raise.

19. Tailplane provides:

a. Lateral stability
b. Directional stability
c. Longitudinal stability (pitch)
d. Propulsion
Answer: c. Longitudinal (pitch) stability

Horizontal stabilizer provides longitudinal stability.

20. Verticle fin provides:

a. Lateral stability
b. Directional (yaw) stability
c. Longitudinal stability
d. Lift
Answer: b. Directional (yaw) stability

Vertical fin provides weathercock stability.

21. Stick shaker activates at:

a. Vne
b. Vs
c. Vsw (stick shaker speed)
d. Vc
Answer: c. Slightly above stall speed (Vss)

Stick shaker warns of approaching stall.

22. Mach number is ratio of:

a. TAS / Speed of sound
b. IAS / Speed of sound
c. GS / TAS
d. Pressure ratio
Answer: a. TAS / Local speed of sound

Mach = TAS / a (a varies with temperature).

23. Coffin corner is:

a. Low speed stall boundary
b. High speed stall (low IAS, high altitude)
c. Never exceed speed
d. Best climbing speed
Answer: b. Where low speed stall and high speed buffet meet

Coffin corner = high altitude where IAS range shrinks.

24. Terminal velocity is:

a. Maximum speed in dive
b. Constant speed when drag = weight
c. Never exceed speed
d. Best glide speed
Answer: b. Speed where drag = weight (free fall)

Terminal velocity = constant when forces balance.

25. Laminar flow wing has:

a. Forward max thickness
b. Aft max thickness
c. Symmetric airfoil
d. High camber
Answer: a. Forward max thickness (laminar bucket)

Laminar flow wings have max thickness further aft.

26. Wake turbulence is strongest with:

a. Light aircraft, high speed
b. Heavy aircraft, low speed
c. Light aircraft, low speed
d. All aircraft same
Answer: b. Heavy, clean (low speed = high AoA = strong vortices)

Wake strength ∝ weight × speed × wingspan.

27. Spin is:

a. Steep turn
b. Stall + yaw (autorotation)
c. Spiral dive
d. Level flight
Answer: b. Stall + yawing (one wing stalled, aircraft autorotates)

Spin = stall + asymmetric lift causing autorotation.

28. To recover from spin:

a. Increase power
b. Reduce power, opposite rudder, relax backpressure
c. Pull back
d. Aileron into spin
Answer: b. Throttle idle, opposite rudder, relax backpressure

PARES: Power idle, Aileron neutral, Rudder opposite, Elevator (relax), Slight nose up.

29. Maneuvering speed (Va):

a. Never exceed
b. Full deflection won't cause structural damage
c. Best climb
d. Landing speed
Answer: b. Speed where full control deflection = limit load factor

At Va, aircraft stalls before exceeding limit load.

30. Service ceiling is:

a. Max altitude
b. 100 fpm climb
c. 500 fpm climb
d. Absolute ceiling
Answer: b. Altitude where climb rate = 100 fpm

Absolute ceiling = 0 fpm climb.

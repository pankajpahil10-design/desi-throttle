Q1. VOR stands for:

a) Visual Omni Range
b) VHF Omni Range
c) Very High Frequency Omnidirectional Range
d) Visual Omnidirectional Radio
Answer: c) VHF Omnidirectional Range

VOR operates in VHF band (108-118MHz) and provides radial information to/from the station.

Q2. DME measures:

a) Magnetic heading
b) Slant range distance
c) Ground speed
d) Altitude
Answer: b) Slant range distance

DME measures slant range - the direct line distance from aircraft to ground station.

Q3. ILS provides:

a) Distance only
b) Direction only
c) Both direction and distance
d) Weather information
Answer: c) Both direction and distance

ILS has localizer (horizontal guidance), glide slope (vertical guidance), and marker beacons for distance.

Q4. NDB operates in:

a) VHF band
b) UHF band
c) LF/MF band (190-1750 kHz)
d) SHF band
Answer: c) LF/MF band (190-1750 kHz)

NDBs operate in Low/Medium Frequency band and are used for ADF navigation.

Q5. GPS uses:

a) Ground-based stations
b) Satellite constellation (24+ satellites)
c) Radio waves only
d) Radar
Answer: b) Satellite constellation (24+ satellites)

GPS uses 24+ satellites in 6 orbital planes to provide position, velocity, and time information.

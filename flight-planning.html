1. Standard filing time for Flight Plan:

a. 15 min before ETA
b. 30 min before EOBT
c. 1 hour before EOBT
d. 2 hours before EOBT
Answer: b. 30 min before EOBT

File flight plan at least 30 minutes before Estimated Off-Block Time.

2. Alternate aerodrome required when:

a. VFR flight
b. IFR with weather below landing minima
c. Day VFR under 5000ft
d. All flights
Answer: b. When destination weather is below landing minima

IFR flights require alternate when destination not above minima.

3. Minimum fuel for IFR flight:

a. Trip fuel only
b. Trip + 30 min reserve
c. Trip + alternate + reserve
d. Trip + 45 min reserve
Answer: c. Trip fuel + alternate fuel + 30 min reserve

Minimum fuel = trip + alternate + 30 min reserve.

4. Critical fuel scenario:

a. 45 min fuel remaining
b. Fuel to reach destination only
c. Cannot reach destination with safe margin
d. Above reserve fuel
Answer: c. When fuel less than required to reach destination

Critical = cannot reach destination with normal reserves.

5. MEL stands for:

a. Minimum Equipment List
b. Maximum Equipment List
c. Maintenance Equipment List
d. Master Equipment List
Answer: a. Minimum Equipment List

MEL = minimum equipment required for flight.

6. RVSM airspace:

a. Below FL290
b. FL290 - FL410
c. Above FL410
d. All altitudes
Answer: b. RVSM = Reduced Vertical Separation Minimum (FL290-FL410)

RVSM = 1000ft vertical separation instead of 2000ft.

7. PNR stands for:

a. Point of No Return
b. Pilot Name Required
c. Position Not Recorded
d. Pre-navigation Report
Answer: a. Point of No Return

PNR = point where remaining fuel = fuel to destination.

8. ETP is:

a. Estimated Time Position
b. Equal Time Point
c. Estimated Take-off Position
d. Entry Time Parameter
Answer: b. Equal Time Point

ETP = point where time to destination = time to alternate.

9. NOTAM stands for:

a. Notice to Airmen
b. Notice to All Men
c. Navigation to Airmen
d. Night Operations to Airmen
Answer: a. Notice to Airmen

NOTAM = notices containing info about hazards, facilities.

10. SIGMET warning:

a. Routine weather
b. Significant weather hazards
c. Runway closure
d. Navigation aid failure
Answer: b. Significant weather hazards (CB, turbulence, icing)

SIGMET = significant meteorological warnings.

11. METAR is:

a. Forecast
b. Actual weather report
c. Warning
d. Satellite image
Answer: b. METAR = Routine meteorological report (actual conditions)

METAR = current weather at aerodrome.

12. TAF is:

a. Current weather
b. Terminal Aerodrome Forecast (2-9 hours)
c. Past weather
d. Warning
Answer: b. Terminal Aerodrome Forecast (2-9 hour forecast)

TAF = weather forecast for approach/departure.

13. ATS route:

a. Any route
b. Designated Air Traffic Service route
c. Random route
d. Training route
Answer: b. Air Traffic Service designated route

ATS = established airway/route with ATC service.

14. ICAO flight plan items:

a. Aircraft type, registration, route
b. Only route
c. Only fuel
d. Passenger list
Answer: a. Aircraft type, registration, route, fuel, persons on board

Flight plan includes aircraft, route, times, fuel, POB.

15. RPL:

a. Random Flight Plan
b. Repetitive Flight Plan
c. Reduced Flight Plan
d. Route Flight Plan
Answer: b. Repetitive Flight Plan

RPL = filed for regular scheduled flights.

16. Fuel endurance:

a. Maximum distance
b. Maximum time aircraft can fly
c. Takeoff fuel
d. Reserve fuel
Answer: b. Maximum time aircraft can fly on fuel available

Endurance = total fuel / fuel flow = time.

17. Holding fuel calculation:

a. 30 min at holding speed
b. 45 min at holding speed
c. 1 hour at holding speed
d. Based on alternate distance
Answer: a. 30 min at holding speed (or as per CAR)

Holding fuel = 30 min at appropriate holding speed/altitude.

18. Alternate fuel:

a. Distance to alternate
b. Distance + approach + miss + 30 min
c. Just trip fuel
d. Zero for VFR
Answer: b. Distance to alternate + approach + miss + 30 min reserve

Alternate fuel = fuel to fly to + 30 min reserve.

19. VFR weather minima:

a. 1 km visibility
b. 5 km visibility, cloud clearances
c. No minima
d. 10 km
Answer: b. 5 km visibility, clear of cloud below 1000ft

Basic VFR = 5km, cloud clearances.

20. IFR weather minima:

a. No cloud separation
b. 1000ft above MDH/MDA
c. 500ft
d. Same as VFR
Answer: b. 1000ft above DH/DA or MDA

IFR = maintain altitude at or above decision height/altitude.

21. CNS/ATM:

a. Communication, Navigation, Surveillance/Air Traffic Management
b. Control, Network, System
c. Crew, Navigation, Safety
d. Combined Navigation Approach
Answer: a. Communication, Navigation, Surveillance/Air Traffic Management

CNS/ATM = modern aviation navigation system.

22. RVR:

a. Runway Visual Range
b. Radar Visual Range
c. Required Visual Range
d. Radio Visual Range
Answer: a. Runway Visual Range (visibility along runway)

RVR measured by transmissometer at touchdown zone.

23. Takeoff alternate required when:

a. Always
b. If takeoff visibility below minima
c. Only at night
d. Never required
Answer: b. When runway visibility below takeoff minima

Takeoff alternate needed if cannot return to departure aerodrome.

24. EOBT:

a. Estimated On-Block Time
b. Estimated Off-Block Time
c. End of Block Time
d. Engine On Before Takeoff
Answer: b. Estimated Off-Block Time

EOBT = time aircraft expects to vacate parking stand.

25. ATC clearance includes:

a. Call sign only
b. Clearance limit, route, altitude, squawk
c. Fuel load only
d. Weather info
Answer: b. Clearance limit, route, altitude, squawk code, departure frequency

Read back clearance items requiring confirmation.

26. Cold temperature correction:

a. Add to altitudes
b. Apply cold temperature correction table
c. No correction needed
d. Subtract from altitude
Answer: b. Add height when temperature below ISA (cold = true altitude lower)

Below ISA, add correction to published altitudes.

27. LMT:

a. Local Mean Time
b. Landing Management Time
c. Lower Meridian Time
d. Last Military Time
Answer: a. Local Mean Time (time at specific longitude)

LMT = solar time at location.

28. Groundspeed calculation:

a. TAS + wind
b. TAS - wind component
c. IAS + wind
d. TAS
Answer: b. TAS + headwind component OR TAS - tailwind component

GS = TAS +/- wind component.

29. 1 nm equals:

a. 6080 feet
b. 5280 feet
c. 7000 feet
d. 1852 meters
Answer: a. 6080 feet (1852 meters)

1 NM = 1 minute of latitude.

30. Mach number is:

a. IAS / Speed of sound
b. TAS / Local speed of sound
c. GS / Speed of sound
d. Weight / Speed
Answer: b. TAS / Local speed of sound

Mach = TAS √∑ speed of sound (varies with temperature).
